once a month, feel your melons. 40% of diagnosed breast cancers are discovered by women who feel a lump
Performing breast self-exams is important, as it is how almost half of diagnosed breast cancers get noticed.
Frequent breast self-exams help you become familiar with your breasts, which helps you see or feel a change in your breasts.
You should not only rely on breast self-exams to be proactive about your breast health. Mammograms can discover tumors before they can be felt. However, when combined altogether along with proper medical care, breast self-exams are a great tool to have in order for you to report noticed changes to your doctor.

how should I perform a breast self-exam?

#1

in the shower.

Using the pads of your three middle fingers, examine the whole armpit and breast area by pressing down with light, medium and firm amounts of pressure. Check for any lumps, hardened knot, thickening or any other changes in your breasts.

#2

in front of a mirror.

To begin, visually examine your breasts while your arms are at your sides. Then, raise your arms high above your head. Look for any changes in your breasts such as swelling, skin dimpling, contour or even nipple changes. After that, place your palms on your hips and press firmly to flex your chest muscles. Look for any puckering, dimpling or changes, especially on one side (as breasts do not often exactly match).

#3

lying down.

Lying down helps to evenly spread out breast tissue. Place a pillow under your right shoulder and your right arm behind your head. Use your left hand to move the pads of your fingers around your right breast. Be sure to cover the whole armpit and breast area. Use light, medium, and firm pressure. Squeeze the nipple to check for lumps and discharge, and repeat all of these steps for your left breast.

how else should I be proactive?

In addition to self-examining your own breasts each month, be sure to tell your doctor if you notice any changes. Continue to have regular visits with your doctor to get a professional breast exam, any testing and mammographies. The key to a happy chest is frequently checking up on your breasts!
For more information, please visit nationalbreastcancer.org where you can learn more and find additional resources about breast cancer.
